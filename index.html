How to read the odds. Each estimate compares the entered GPA and SAT against the school's typical admitted profile, then nudges the result tougher because engineering is admitted more selectively than the university overall. It's a planning heuristic to help you sort reach from likely, not an official prediction, and it can't see essays, recommendations, or your junior-year upward trend, all of which matter.

About the figures. Cost is an estimated full cost of attendance, meaning tuition, fees, and room and board, for an out-of-state student before any aid. Size is approximate undergraduate enrollment, and the SAT ranges are approximate middle-50 bands. Verify the exact numbers on each school's admissions page or Common Data Set before making decisions. IE marks schools with a dedicated industrial engineering degree; ME marks mechanical engineering. Starred schools, interest ratings, and your inputs are saved in this browser, so they're still here when you come back on this device. The × on a card removes a school you'd never consider, and "Removed" up top lets you review those and bring any back.

Engineering vs General. The applicant switch shows the gap between applying straight into engineering and applying to the university generally. Engineering admits run tougher, so the odds drop in engineering mode. The gap is the price of applying direct, and the transfer-in indicator is the alternative.

Transfer in from general. Shown only in engineering mode, this rates how realistic it is to start undeclared or in another major and switch into engineering later. Defined means a published GPA-and-coursework route that isn't capacity-capped, Competitive means a real but space-limited fight, First-year general means everyone starts in general engineering and picks a major after year one, and Usually open means switching is generally feasible if you handle the intro calculus and physics. Only UMass is marked verified, from its published policy; the rest are general reads on how each school admits engineers, so confirm the current rule with each school.

Discover more schools. The search box queries the U.S. Department of Education's College Scorecard, the official federal college database. It needs a free API key and works once the page is online. Results come straight from that data, which doesn't include GPA or drive time, so those show as not available and the odds rely on selectivity and any reported SAT range. Treat discovered schools as leads to research, not vetted picks.

Distance and campus look. The distance slider sets how far from South Orange to consider, and widening it also widens the states the search covers. The campus rating is a rough, subjective read from general reputation, so use "See photos" and ideally a visit to judge for yourself.
